Controlled language

The terms we use for certain concepts, and why we use them.


Account - The employer’s account. We don’t call it levy account because it’s not just an account for levy, it’s a ledger of non-levy transactions too.

Add an apprentice - The action of creating a commitment for an apprentice.

Agreement - The legal agreement signed between the ESFA and an employer, employer-provider or training provider.

Agreement not signed / Agreement signed - Employers can only spend funds on behalf of their organisation if that organisation has a signed agreement.

Apprenticeship Assessment Organisation - The organisation responsible for assessing the learning and skills of an apprentice as a result of their apprenticeship.

Apprenticeship training course - The training given by the training provider to the apprentice. This could be either for an apprenticeship standard or a framework.

Approve - Both employers and providers ‘approve’ cohorts once they’re happy that all the necessary apprentices have been added and their details are correct. This approval is authorisation for the release of funds.

Authorise payments to training providers - The release of levy funds from an employer’s account to a training provider.

Bulk upload - The ability to add details of multiple apprentices to your account at the same time by uploading a csv file.

Changes for review - Changes made to an apprentice’s details, by another party, that you need to review and then either approve or reject.

Changes pending - Changes that you’ve made to an apprentice’s details, that another party need to review and either approve or reject.

Cohort - One or more apprentices being entered into the apprenticeship service at the same time, all part of the same contract for services between a legal entity and training provider.

Co-investment - Policy mechanism where government pays 90% of the cost of an apprenticeship, and the employer pays the remaining 10%.

Co-investment you've gained - Term used as a behavioural nudge to help frame the spending of funds as a gain.

Committed spend - The funds that will be taken out of an employer’s account according to the commitments they’ve created.

Connected organisations - An organisation is ‘connected’ to your organisation if your organisation has control of it or if both organisations are controlled by the same people, such as a board of directors.

Contract - A contract for services between an employer and a training provider.

Current funds - The current amount of levy funds in an employer’s account.

End-point assessments - The assessment that takes place towards the end of an apprenticeship to make sure each apprentice has reached the necessary level to be awarded an apprenticeship.

End-point assessors - The individuals or organisations carrying out end-point assessments on apprentices.

English percentage / English % - Spell out the term in sentences and use the % symbol when using it for tabular data . The English percentage is provided by HMRC based on the data in the employer’s payroll submissions. It represents the proportion of the employer’s payroll that's paid to their employees that live in England.

ESFA - Spell it out on first instance including the word ‘the’, eg contact the Education and Skills Funding Agency (ESFA). Subsequently, use the acronym and don’t use the word ‘the’, eg contact ESFA.

Expiring funds / Use funds - The funds that will disappear from the employer’s account according to sunsetting rules. If funds aren’t spent, they expire after 24 months. Term used as a behavioural nudge. 'Use funds' is the CTA for spending the expiring funds. This term is used as a behavioural nudge.

Financial data - The raw financial data that employers can download from their account.

Funds credit your account - We say funds credit the account. This is a fast way to give employers immediate understanding of what’s happening. We don’t say ‘enter the account’ because money doesn’t enter anything, the account is a digital representation of credit.

Funding band maximum - The upper limit that can be contributed from levy funds to pay for an individual apprenticeship training course (including assessment). The training provider will need to invoice employers for any amounts above this limit.

ILR data mismatch - A difference in the data held in the apprenticeship service and the data held in the ILR which could result in a payment failure if not resolved.

Incentives - Terms for the incentive payments that employers receive for taking on certain types of apprenticeships.

Incentives you've gained - Term used as a behavioural nudge to help frame the spending of funds as a gain.

Live - An apprentice that has started their apprenticeship training course.

Levy funds - The funds that are in an employer’s account.

Levy paid to HMRC - The levy funds that were taken via the employer’s payroll by HMRC.

Levy payer - An organisation, or group of connected organisations, that has collective payroll above £3 million and therefore pays the levy.

Organisation - The legal entity that's named on the contract for services with the training provider.

Overlapping training dates - Two records for the same apprentice (based on unique learner number) that show the apprentice on two apprenticeship training courses at the same time. Note that start and end dates don’t need to match - the overlap could just be for a portion of the training period.

Paused - An apprentice who’s temporarily stopped their training, possibly due to illness. There won’t be any payments leaving the account for this apprentice, although this can be undone.

Pay bill - The total amount of money employers pay their employees each year. Most employers refer to their 'payroll' rather than 'pay bill'.

Payment schedule - A summary of the anticipated cost breakdown for a cohort across the duration of their learning.

Resume - The ability to re-start an apprentice who’s currently in the apprenticeship service in a ‘paused’ state.

Spending status - Label for whether or not an organisation has a signed agreement and can spend funds.

Spending disabled - There’s no signed agreement for an employer organisation.

Spending enabled - There is a signed agreement for an employer organisation.

Stopped - An apprentice who’s left their apprenticeship training course. This may be because they’ve left the company. There won’t be any payments leaving the account for this apprentice.

Team member / Your team - Terms for an employer’s own staff members or colleagues.

Total agreed apprenticeship price - How much the training provider is charging the employer for the apprentice’s training, including both the training and assessment elements of the apprenticeship.

Waiting to start - An apprentice whose records have been approved by both the employer and provider within the apprenticeship service, but who hasn’t reached the planned training start date for their apprenticeship yet.
