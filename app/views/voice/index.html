Voice and tone

How and when we add a human touch to our interfaces.

The main thing about voice and tone is users shouldn't notice it, and it shouldn’t get in the way of users doing their task.

You can use the following voice and tone tables to help you find the right words to match the context you’re writing for.

Voice and tone tables

For routine transactions our voice is clear and to-the-point
Write like this: Enter organisation name
Not like this: Please tell us which organisation you want to add
Why? We aim to reduce the cognitive burder on users wherever possible. Don't make them read more thnn they need to.

In-service our voice is informative and tells users what they can do
Write like this: Payment failed because some details are missing or incorrect. Check Yuval's details and contact Acme training ltd.
Not like this: Sorry, your payment failed and we're not really sure why.
Why? We present the information users need at the exact point in time and exact page that they need it.

In-service our voice is positive and supportive
Write like this: Agreement signed - Good Content ltd can now spend levy funds
Not like this: Agreement signed - return to homepage
Why? We use positive feedback to reassure users that they're on the right track, giving them confidence and peace of mind.

In-service our voice is professional and competent
Write like this: Funds will credit your account from any PAYE schemes you add. No funds will credit your account until May 2017.
Not like this: Funds will hit your account around May 2017.
Why? We're handling sensitive corporate information and data, and it's important that user's trust and have confidence in ESFA as an agency.

In-service our voice is personal, but not too personal
Write like this: Good morning, welcome to your account.
Not like this: Hey Pete, welcome to your account.
Why? Just because we know the user's name doesn't mean we should always use it. Some people prefer to be addressed by their surname or their title, eg Dr. Jekyll.

For emails our voice is more personal
Write like this: Dear Pete, Here’s you access code... kind regards
Not like this: Dear user, This email contains your access code...
Why? As opposed to interfaces, emails are a more legitimate form of dialogue and are more intimate. Using the user's name also helps reduce the risk of emails being flagged up as spam by organisations' firewalls.

For help and support our voice is neutral and direct
Write like this: The PAYE scheme for the Government Gateway details you entered is already added to an account.
Not like this: Sorry, PAYE scheme not added! Check that it's not already added to your account.
Why? When things go wrong, users need to know why. Best to avoid any unecessary personality in help messages.
